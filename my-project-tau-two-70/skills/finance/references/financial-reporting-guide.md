# Financial Reporting Guide

A comprehensive guide for producing professional financial deliverables,
including report templates, document structures, and key metric definitions.

## 5. Financial Model Documentation Best Practices

### Structure
Every financial model should include a documentation cover sheet or appendix with:

1. **Model Purpose**: Clear statement of what the model is designed to evaluate.
2. **Assumption Log**: Tabular list of all key assumptions with sources and dates.
3. **Version History**: Track changes, authors, and dates for each iteration.
4. **Data Sources**: List all data providers and specific feeds used.
5. **Formula Documentation**: Annotated formula map for complex calculations.

### Assumption Log Template
| Assumption | Value | Source | Last Updated | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue growth (Y1) | 12.0% | Management guidance + consensus | [Date] | High |
| Terminal growth rate | 2.5% | Long-term GDP estimate | [Date] | Medium |
| WACC | 9.5% | CAPM model (see calc tab) | [Date] | Medium |

### Modeling Standards
- **Color coding**: Blue = hard-coded input; black = formula; green = link to other sheet.
- **Error checks**: Dedicated error-check tab verifying balance sheet balances, circular references resolved, and key ratios within reasonable bounds.
- **Sensitivity analysis**: Always include data tables for key output variables (NPV, IRR, share price) across ranges of critical assumptions.
- **Scenario modeling**: At minimum, build Base, Bull, and Bear cases with clearly defined assumption sets.
- **Time horizon consistency**: Use consistent time periods across all model tabs. Clearly label columns with dates.
- **Documentation depth**: Every formula with more than 3 components should have an adjacent comment explaining the logic.

## 6. Key Financial Metrics Definitions and Formulas

### Profitability Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| ROE | Net Income / Average Shareholders' Equity | Measures how efficiently a company generates profit from shareholders' equity. The single most important profitability metric for equity investors. |
| ROA | Net Income / Average Total Assets | Measures how efficiently a company uses its assets to generate profit. Useful for comparing capital-intensive vs. asset-light business models. |
| EBITDA | Operating Income + Depreciation + Amortization | Proxy for operating cash flow before capital structure and accounting decisions. Standardized for cross-company comparisons. |

**EBITDA Margin**: `EBITDA / Revenue x 100` — Normalizes profitability for comparison across companies with different depreciation policies and debt levels.

### Growth Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| CAGR | (Ending Value / Beginning Value)^(1/n) - 1 | Compound Annual Growth Rate. Smooths multi-year growth into a single annualized rate. Preferred over simple average for multi-period analysis. |
| Revenue CAGR | (Revenue_n / Revenue_0)^(1/n) - 1 | Annualized revenue growth over n periods. |
| EPS CAGR | (EPS_n / EPS_0)^(1/n) - 1 | Annualized earnings-per-share growth. |

**Example CAGR Calculation:**
```
Revenue grows from $100M to $150M over 4 years.
CAGR = (150/100)^(1/4) - 1 = 1.5^0.25 - 1 = 1.1067 - 1 = 10.67% per year
```

### Investment Return Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| NPV | Sum[CF_t / (1+r)^t] - Initial Investment | Net Present Value. Sum of all future cash flows discounted to present value minus the initial investment. NPV > 0 means the investment creates value. |
| IRR | Rate at which NPV = 0 | Internal Rate of Return. The discount rate that makes NPV exactly zero. IRR > cost of capital means value creation. Best used alongside NPV for decision-making. |
| WACC | (E/V x Re) + (D/V x Rd x (1-T)) | Weighted Average Cost of Capital. Blended cost of all capital sources (equity and debt). Serves as the hurdle rate for investment decisions and the discount rate for DCF models. |

**WACC Component Detail:**
```
Re (Cost of Equity) = Risk-Free Rate + Beta x Equity Risk Premium + Size/Specific Risk Premium
Rd (Cost of Debt) = Yield to maturity on outstanding debt (pre-tax)
T = Corporate tax rate
E = Market value of equity
D = Market value of debt
V = E + D (total firm value)
```

**NPV Decision Rules:**
- NPV > 0: Accept (creates value). NPV < 0: Reject (destroys value). NPV = 0: Indifferent.
- For mutually exclusive projects, choose the one with the highest NPV (not necessarily highest IRR).

**IRR vs. NPV:** IRR can be misleading with non-conventional cash flows (multiple sign changes). IRR assumes reinvestment at IRR rate; NPV assumes reinvestment at WACC (more realistic). Always present both.

## Quick Reference: Report Selection Guide
| Report Type | Frequency | Primary Audience | Key Sections |
|---|---|---|---|
| Quarterly Financial Summary | Quarterly | Investors, analysts | Financials, margins, guidance |
| Investment Thesis | As needed | Investment committee, clients | Thesis, valuation, risks, catalysts |
